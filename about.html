WHY TRADINGVIEW ALERTS FAIL ON ANDROID

TradingView sends alerts as push notifications. Push notifications use Android's notification audio channel — which is silenced whenever your phone is on silent mode or Do Not Disturb is active. If you're sleeping, in a meeting, or just away from the screen, the alert arrives silently. You miss the alert. You miss the trade.

This is a structural limitation of how TradingView delivers alerts on Android. There is no setting inside TradingView to fix it. The notification channel simply cannot override your device's sound settings.

HOW TVIEWALARM DELIVERS LOUD TRADING ALERTS

TviewAlarm bridges TradingView's webhook system with Android's alarm audio channel — the same channel used by your alarm clock, which Android exempts from silent mode and Do Not Disturb by design.

When TradingView fires an alert, it sends a webhook payload to your personal TviewAlarm URL. TviewAlarm receives it and immediately triggers alarm-channel audio on your Android device — at full volume, regardless of your sound settings, every time.

Setup takes under two minutes: install the app, copy your webhook URL, paste it into your TradingView alert. No API keys, no code, no configuration files.
