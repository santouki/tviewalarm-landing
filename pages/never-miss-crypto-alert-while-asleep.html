The Problem Isn't Your Alerts. It's How They're Delivered.

Most traders assume that setting an alert = being alerted. It doesn't. Setting an alert means TradingView will attempt to notify you via push notification when the condition is met. Whether that notification actually reaches you — whether it makes a sound, wakes you up, and gets your attention — depends entirely on your phone's notification settings at that moment.

And if you're sleeping, those settings are almost certainly working against you:

  • ✕
    Do Not Disturb — silences all push notifications while you sleep
  • ✕
    Silent mode — removes sound from notification channel
  • ✕
    Battery optimization — Android delays background push delivery to extend battery life
  • ✕
    App restrictions — some Android manufacturers aggressively kill background apps, including TradingView

The result: your alert fires, TradingView tries to deliver it, and nothing happens on your end. Your setup was correct. The delivery layer failed.
