Why This Keeps Happening — and Why It's Not Your Fault

Push notifications and alarms are fundamentally different things on Android. Push notifications — the kind TradingView sends — are designed for information, not interruption. They respect your phone's volume and DND settings.

An alarm — the kind your clock app uses — is a protected audio event. It bypasses volume settings. It bypasses DND. It is specifically designed to wake you up.

The problem is that TradingView doesn't send alarms. It sends notifications. And until you route your alerts through a system that triggers a real alarm, you will keep missing overnight moves.

  • ✕
    Push notifications respect silent mode. Your TradingView alert fires — and your phone stays quiet.
  • ✕
    DND is active while you sleep. Most traders enable it. TradingView notifications are blocked.
  • ✕
    Battery optimization delays delivery. Android queues background notifications to save power at night.
  • ✕
    You can't react to what you didn't hear. The alert was there — you just never knew.
