TradingView Alerts Not Waking Me Up — Here's the Fix

Your alerts are firing. TradingView is doing its job. But your phone stays silent — and you sleep through the trade. Here's exactly why, and how to fix it permanently in under 60 seconds.

Quick Answer

TradingView alerts don't wake you up because they're sent as standard push notifications — which Android silences during Do Not Disturb, battery saver, and low-volume states. The fix is to route alerts through a webhook to a dedicated alarm app like TviewAlarm, which triggers a true alarm-priority sound that bypasses silent mode entirely.

Why Your TradingView Alerts Are Completely Silent

You did everything right. You set the alert. You tested it during the day — it buzzed, it beeped, it showed up. You went to bed thinking you were covered.

Then at 3:14 AM, Bitcoin broke the level you'd been watching for six weeks. TradingView fired the alert. Your phone received it. And you slept straight through a move that would have paid your rent.

This is not a TradingView bug. This is a fundamental Android notification problem. TradingView sends standard push notifications — the same category as emails and Instagram likes. Android treats them identically, which means:

  • ✕
    Do Not Disturb silences them. If you have DND on while sleeping (most traders do), TradingView alerts make zero sound.
  • ✕
    Battery saver blocks them. Android delays or drops push notifications to save battery at night.
  • ✕
    Notification volume ≠ alarm volume. Even at max notification volume, push alerts won't ring as loud as a clock alarm.
  • ✕
    Screen-off = delivery delay. Background app restrictions push TradingView notifications to a queue — they arrive late, or not at all.

What Happens Every Time You Miss an Alert

It's not just the trade you miss in the moment. It's the compounding cost of operating without a safety net:

  • ✕

  • ✕

  • ✕

  • ✕
    Alert anxiety compounds. The longer this goes on, the more you doubt your setup — and start making emotional decisions to compensate.

Silent notifications are not a minor inconvenience. For active traders, they're a structural failure in your risk system.

The Fix: Route Alerts Through a Dedicated Alarm Layer

TradingView has a feature most traders never use: webhooks. Instead of (or in addition to) push notifications, TradingView can send an HTTP request to any URL you specify when an alert fires.

TviewAlarm gives you a personal webhook URL. When TradingView hits that URL, TviewAlarm triggers an alarm — not a notification. A true alarm-priority audio event, using Android's alarm audio channel, which operates completely independently of:

  • ✓
    Do Not Disturb mode — alarm channel bypasses DND by default
  • ✓
    Silent mode — alarm volume is separate from notification and media volume
  • ✓
    Screen off state — high-priority FCM wakes the device before the alarm fires
  • ✓
    Battery optimization — alarm events are exempt from Android's background restrictions
