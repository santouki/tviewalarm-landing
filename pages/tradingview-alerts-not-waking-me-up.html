Why Your TradingView Alerts Don't Wake You Up

Every night, traders set TradingView alerts on their key levels, go to sleep trusting those alerts, and wake up to timestamps showing exactly when the move happened without them. The alert fired. The trade ran. Their phone made no sound.

This is not a TradingView configuration error. It's a fundamental mismatch between how TradingView delivers alerts and what Android allows to make sound while you sleep. Understanding this distinction is the entire fix.

TradingView sends push notifications. Push notifications use Android's notification audio channel. That channel is governed by three restrictions that activate simultaneously while you sleep:

1

Do Not Disturb silences the notification channel

DND blocks all notification audio completely. If you sleep with DND on — which you should — your TradingView alert produces zero sound, no matter what volume your phone is set to.

Kills Sound
2

Android Doze mode delays push delivery

After your screen has been off for several minutes, Android batches push notifications to save battery. Your alert can arrive 5–15 minutes after it fires. On a fast breakout, that entry is gone.

Delays Alert
3

Battery optimization kills TradingView in background

Samsung, Xiaomi, Oppo, and others aggressively terminate background apps overnight. TradingView may not be running at all when your alert fires — meaning no push delivery even to attempt.

Kills App
✓

The alarm channel is exempt from all three restrictions

Android's alarm channel — used by TviewAlarm — bypasses DND, is exempt from Doze delays via high-priority FCM, and cannot be killed by battery optimization. It was designed to wake you up. That's exactly what it does.

Full Bypass
