Quick Answer

TradingView alerts are silenced by silent mode and Do Not Disturb because they use Android's notification channel — which both settings control. The permanent fix: route alerts via TradingView webhook to TviewAlarm, which uses Android's alarm channel. Silent Mode Blocks TradingView. It Doesn't Block Alarms.

Android has two completely separate audio systems that traders need to understand. Silent mode and Do Not Disturb control one of them — and leave the other completely untouched.

Notification Channel — Controlled by Silent Mode

  • ✕ Silent mode mutes it completely
  • ✕ Do Not Disturb blocks all audio
  • ✕ Notification volume slider controls it
  • ✕ Used by TradingView push alerts
  • ✕ Used by email, social, all apps
  • ✕ Will not ring while you sleep

Alarm Channel — Exempt from Silent Mode

  • ✓ Silent mode cannot touch it
  • ✓ Do Not Disturb cannot block it
  • ✓ Separate alarm volume slider
  • ✓ Used by your alarm clock app
  • ✓ Used by TviewAlarm
  • ✓ Rings at full volume while you sleep

TviewAlarm moves your TradingView alerts from the notification channel to the alarm channel. That's the entire fix. Your phone's silent mode, DND, and volume settings stop mattering entirely for the alerts you care about.
