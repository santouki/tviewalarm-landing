Why Silent Mode Blocks Your Trading Alerts

Android manages sound through multiple distinct audio channels. Most people assume "silent mode" silences everything. It doesn't — it specifically targets the notification and media channels, while leaving the alarm channel untouched.

This is by design: you want your 6AM alarm to ring even if you put your phone on silent the night before. Your alarm clock app uses the alarm channel. Your phone call ringtone can be configured to use it too.

The problem is that almost every alert app — including TradingView's own push notifications — uses the notification channel.

  • ✕ Blocked by Do Not Disturb
  • ✕ Delayed by battery saver
  • ✕ Used by TradingView push
  • ✕ Used by most alert apps

Alarm Channel

  • ✓ Bypasses silent mode
  • ✓ Bypasses Do Not Disturb
  • ✓ Exempt from battery saver

TviewAlarm routes your TradingView alerts through the alarm channel. This isn't a hack or workaround — it's using the audio system exactly as Android designed it. Your trades are as important as your morning alarm. They deserve the same delivery priority.
